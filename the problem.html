THE PROBLEM

1

WHY
THIS PROBLEM MATTERS

Current excavation systems still face challenges in ensuring consistent artifact protection and accurate recovery, especially in large or complex dig sites. The lack of automated detection and handling systems increases the possibility of artifact damage and reduces excavation efficiency. These challenges highlight the need for improved excavation support systems that can assist archaeologists in maintaining precision while preserving historical materials.
2

WHO
IS AFFECTED

This problem affects archaeologists, researchers, historians, and preservation specialists who rely on accurate artifact recovery for historical study. It also affects museums, educational institutions, and future researchers who depend on well-preserved artifacts and accurate excavation records. In addition, local communities and society are affected because damaged or lost artifacts can result in the loss of important cultural and historical knowledge.
3

WHAT
ARE THE CHALLENGES

Modern excavation suffers from a reliance on manual techniques and outdated technology, resulting in inefficient workflows and frequent operational errors. These traditional methods risk damaging delicate artifacts and complicate data analysis through inconsistent, non-automated documentation. Furthermore, the absence of innovation forces archaeologists to work in hazardous conditions, compromising both their personal safety and overall performance.
