EXPLORING what lies beneath

Exploring what lies beneath the ground is essential for understanding human history and preserving cultural heritage. Archaeology allows researchers to study past civilizations, environments, and technological developments through artifacts buried underground. Protecting these discoveries ensures that future generations can continue to learn from history.

CHALLENGES in Archaeology

Traditional archaeological excavation relies heavily on manual labor, making the process slow, physically demanding, and unsafe. In large dig sites, artifacts may also be misidentified during sorting due to human error, fatigue, and harsh environmental conditions. In addition, documentation is often done manually, making long-term tracking and analysis more difficult.

REAL-WORLD relevance

As excavation sites continue to expand, archaeologists face increasing challenges in uncovering artifacts accurately while protecting historical layers and ensuring proper preservation. These highlight the need for safer, more reliable, and more efficient excavation methods that can improve accuracy.

CONNECTION to FLL theme

This real-world problem directly connects to the FLL Unearthed challenge, which focuses on finding innovative ways to explore the underground while protecting history and improving how excavation is performed. The theme encourages teams to develop solutions through the use of technology and engineering.
