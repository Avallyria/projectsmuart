INSIGHTS

RESEARCHING FINDINGS

Working on SMUART taught us the importance of conducting thorough background research and developing a deep understanding of real-world archaeological challenges before attempting to create a solution. Through this process, we learned how to review existing excavation techniques, study artifact-handling methods, and identify gaps or limitations in current approaches. This helped us understand the specific difficulties that archaeologists face, such as carefully handling fragile artifacts, sorting large numbers of items efficiently, and working in unsafe or unstable dig sites.


In order to better replicate meticulous excavation and artifact management, we were able to apply study findings directly to our design by concentrating on these real-world archaeological difficulties. This allowed us to make well-informed decisions regarding the robot's mechanics, sensor integration, and workflow. This technique made it easier for us to relate theory to reality and made it clear how crucial evidence-based innovation is to the preservation of historical assets. In the end, thorough investigation made sure that SMUART is consistent with the FLL Unearthed theme and is both technically possible and pertinent to the difficulties of discovering and safeguarding what is buried underneath.

PROBLEM-SOLVING SKILLS

Through multiple iterations and hands-on testing, our team encountered a variety of technical and design challenges, including stabilizing the robot, securing the conveyor system, and safely handling simulated fragile artifacts. Each challenge required careful observation, critical analysis, and thoughtful experimentation to identify the root causes and determine effective solutions. By approaching problems systematically, we learned to break down complex issues into smaller, manageable tasks and test possible solutions in a controlled and organized manner.


With each iteration, we were able to improve the robot's programming logic and mechanical design, guaranteeing more accurate excavation, artifact detection, and sorting performance. By experimenting with different strategies after the first ones failed, including altering the robot's structure or moving the sensors, we also improved our ability to think creatively. We gained perseverance, patience, and resilience by overcoming these obstacles, which reaffirmed the need of learning from errors. In the end, our problem-solving skills improved our capacity to effectively address technological challenges and raise the SMUART system's overall dependability and performance.
